What Is Baptism?

Declaration of Faith

Baptism is a public declaration of your decision to follow Jesus. At MBC, a pastor or trusted friend stands with you at the baptism pool and assists you in the ritual of entering the water and emerging renewed.

Symbolism

The Deeper Meaning

Going underwater symbolizes Jesus’ death and the washing away of sins. Rising out of the water represents the new life granted through His resurrection.

This act is a powerful, public testimony of your renewed commitment and transformation.

Jesus' Baptism

Divine Example

Jesus requested John to baptize Him at the Jordan River. Despite John’s hesitation, Jesus insisted, declaring that this was the moment of setting things right.

As Jesus emerged from the water, the heavens opened, the Spirit descended like a dove, and a voice proclaimed: “This is my Son…” (Matthew 3:13-17 MSG)

Reflect & Imagine

Personal Reflection

Reread the story and imagine yourself in the water, experiencing God’s approval and the gentle presence of the Holy Spirit.

Great Commission

Call to Action

Jesus commanded: “Go out and train everyone you meet, marking them by baptism in the threefold name: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.” (Matthew 28:19 MSG)

Your baptism is a public celebration of your decision to follow Him—a chance to invite your family, friends, and the MBC community to join in this special moment.

How & When It Happens

Practical Details

At MBC, baptism usually takes place during our Sunday service. We invite you to step into the water, knowing that there is flexibility for those who feel anxious.

Please wear suitable clothing (non–see-through when wet) and remember to bring a towel, a change of clothes, and a bag for the wet items. You will also prepare your testimony—whether as a short passage, video clip, or read by someone else.
