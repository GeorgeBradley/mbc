What Is Baptism?

A public way Jesus followers declare their decision to follow Him. A pastor or trusted friend stands with you in a baptism pool (or other water), immersing you as you lean back and rise up—symbolizing Jesus’ death, resurrection, and new life. We celebrate with prayer, cheering, and clapping from your MBC Church family!

Why Baptism?

It’s a symbol of Jesus’ death, resurrection, and new life through Him. Leaning back under the water signifies your sins being washed away. Being raised out of the water reflects your new life—like Jesus’ own resurrection. This public declaration mirrors how Jesus Himself was baptized.

Jesus’ Baptism

“Jesus then appeared, arriving at the Jordan River… John baptized Him, and the skies opened, with God’s Spirit descending like a dove and a voice saying, ‘This is my Son, chosen and marked by my love, delight of my life.’ — Matthew 3:13-17 MSG”

Reread the story and imagine yourself in that moment. How does it feel to hear God’s approval?

How & When It Happens

Baptism usually takes place during a Sunday morning service at Milton Baptist Church. Your MBC family will celebrate, support, and pray for you from that day onward.

What to Bring

  • Towel
  • Change of clothes
  • Bag for wet clothes

Wear clothing that’s suitable for getting wet and is not see-through. If you’re anxious about being at the front or going underwater, don’t worry—there’s flexibility in how we do it.

Jesus’ Mission for Baptism

“Go out and train everyone you meet, far and near, in this way of life, marking them by baptism in the threefold name: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.” — Matthew 28:19 MSG

When you’ve said yes to Jesus’ way, baptism is a clear next step the Bible encourages. At Milton Baptist Church, we celebrate this step as a joyful, public declaration of your faith.

Your Baptism Testimony

Prepare your personal story of how and why you felt called to be baptized. This could be a short written passage, a video clip, or read by someone else if you prefer—it’s flexible. Reflect on your journey with Jesus and how baptism is a meaningful new beginning.

Reflection Questions

  • What does baptism mean to you personally?
  • How has Jesus changed your life?
