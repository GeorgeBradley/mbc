What Is Baptism?

Baptism is a public declaration of your decision to follow Jesus. A pastor or trusted friend assists you in entering the water and coming up, accompanied by prayer, cheering, and clapping.

Symbolism

Going underwater represents Jesus’ death and the washing away of sins. Rising out of the water signifies the new life that comes through His resurrection.

Jesus' Baptism

Jesus insisted on being baptized by John. As He rose from the water, the heavens opened, the Spirit descended like a dove, and a voice declared: “This is my Son, chosen and marked by my love” (Matthew 3:13-17 MSG).

Reflect & Imagine

Reread the story and imagine yourself as the one being baptized. What is the water like? How does it feel to hear God’s approval and sense the Holy Spirit's presence?

Great Commission

At the end of His time on Earth, Jesus charged His followers: “Go out and train everyone... marking them by baptism in the threefold name: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit” (Matthew 28:19 MSG). Your baptism is a public declaration of your new life.

How & When

Baptism typically occurs during a Sunday service. Wear appropriate clothing (not see-through when wet) and remember to bring a towel, a change of clothes, and a bag for the wet ones. You'll also prepare your testimony, whether in writing, video, or read by someone else.
