What Is Baptism?

A public way Jesus followers declare their decision to follow Him. A pastor or trusted friend stands with you in a baptism pool (or other water), immersing you as you lean back and rise up—symbolizing Jesus’ death, resurrection, and new life. We celebrate with prayer, cheering, and clapping from your MBC Church family!

Why Baptism?

It’s a symbol of Jesus’ death, resurrection, and new life through Him. Leaning back under the water signifies your sins being washed away. Being raised out of the water reflects your new life, much like Jesus’ resurrection.

Jesus’ Baptism

“Jesus then appeared, arriving at the Jordan River… John baptized Him, and the skies opened, with God’s Spirit descending like a dove and a voice saying, ‘This is my Son, chosen and marked by my love, delight of my life.’ — Matthew 3:13-17 MSG”

Reflect on this: What does the water feel like? How do you perceive God’s affirmation in that moment?

How & When It Happens

Baptism usually takes place during a Sunday morning service at Milton Baptist Church. Your church family will support and pray for you throughout your journey with God.

What to Bring

  • Towel
  • Change of clothes
  • Bag for wet clothes

Wear clothing that’s suitable for getting wet and not see-through. If you’re anxious about being in front or going underwater, don’t worry—there’s flexibility in our approach.
